✎ Map of Rus' (1054-1132)

In the map above, the principalities of Rus' are shown. These provinces are the following: Novgorod, Polotsk, Volyn, Kyiv, Halych, Pereyaslavl, Rostov-Suzdal, Ryazan, and Smolensk.

✎ Relations with Foreign Countries

At the time of the map being shown above, powerful empires such as the Holy Roman Empire and the Byzantine Empire already existed. Rus' frequently traded with the Byzantines, and their religion, Eastern Orthodox, was influenced by the empire. For more information, check out the Religion and Foreign Relations sections for more.

✎ Russia in the Modern Age

As visible in the map above, the modern territory of Rus' is spread over Russia, Belarus, and Ukraine, with Kyiv being in and the capital of the latter. Like its roots, Russia is still very multicultural with many ethnicities and religions, and still is a mainly Christian.
