The Origins of Russia

Russia is the largest country in the world. It is known for being majorly involved in many historical events and for its unique culture. But where exactly did Russia come from? What are its roots, and who paved the way for its growth and development? Today, you may learn all that from this site. Know of the major events and historical figures that shaped what it was once, Kievan Rus’, through this page and the next.

Importance in History

Russia has been involved in events such as the Cold War, the rise of the Soviet Union, and the rule of the Romanov dynasty. Many local and international events within the country have shaped history through policies, governments, and cultures. It also has its major influence in social and economic structures. Known for being a communist nation, the country has always been in heated situations with the United States of America, a majorly influential capitalist country. Due to this, its system is also quite similar to that of other countries, such as China and Vietnam. Additionally, its monarchy also impacted those of other countries in Europe.

General Timeline

According to Britannica (n.d.), Kievan Rus’ was founded by the Viking Oleg. He was the ruler of Novgorod since 879, one of the oldest Russian cities located in the north. He captured Smolensk and Kiev in 882. Kiev became the capital due to its strategic location. Oleg united local Slavic and Finnish tribes and defeated the Khazars, a semi-nomadic Turkic tribe. He also arranged trade agreements with Constantinople.
His successor was Igor of Kiev, who is considered to be a less capable ruler. He is regarded to be the founder of the Rurik dynasty, who were invited by the people of Novgorod to rule to establish peace and order. His treaty with Constantinople in 945 is considered to be less favorable than the 911 treaty. Igor was also the husband of Saint Olga of Kiev, who attacked the Drevlians after their murder of Igor.
Vladimir the Great was the grandson of Igor. The beginning of the Golden Age of Rus’ was marked by his leadership. One of the most important events that happened during his rule was converting Rus’ to Orthodox Christian. This blossomed from a pact with the then Byzantine Emperor Basil II. Basil II promised Vladimir would marry his sister in exchange for military aid and conversion to Christianity.

Fun Fact: One of the reasons Russia converted to Christianity was their love of alcohol, a beverage which was not banned in the religion. Vladimir the Great even proclaimed, “Drinking is the joy of all Rus’.”
